VorAMR

In computational astrophysics, extraordinarily complicated software suites are required to simulate aspects of the universe. Generations of scientists will adopt, use, contribute to, and pass on their specialized simulation software of choice. A problem arises when enitre research groups become irreparably entwined with unique but isolated software. This can lead to highly unfortunate intellectual quagmires where computational advancements made in one software suite is difficult if not impossible to translate to a another suite due to the computational rigor required.
